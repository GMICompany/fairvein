## Research Methodology

### Ownership Verification
- **Primary source:** Wikipedia articles for L'Oréal, Coty Inc., and Unilever (March 2026)
- **Cross-reference:** Official parent company websites, brand "About Us" pages
- **Acquisition dates:** Noted where significant (e.g., CeraVe 2017, Tatcha 2019)

### Boycott Documentation
- **Standard:** Only included boycotts with news coverage or documented campaigns
- **Sources:** BDS lists, news articles, NGO reports
- **Exclusions:** Speculation, unverified social media claims, general ESG concerns without specific campaigns

### Alternative Brands Selection
- **Criteria:** Genuinely independent (not owned by major conglomerates)
- **Quality bar:** Comparable product category, credible reviews, widely available
- **Diversity:** Mix of price points (drugstore to luxury), ethics (clean, vegan, B-Corp), and ownership (woman-owned, BIPOC-owned, founder-led)

## Known Limitations

1. **Boycott completeness:** This database focuses on well-documented boycotts. There may be valid ethical concerns about brands that don't meet the "documented campaign" threshold.

2. **Ownership changes:** Beauty industry has frequent M&A activity. Verified ownership as of March 2026, but acquisitions may occur.

3. **Indie brand verification:** Some "alternatives" listed may have been acquired since research. Users should verify current ownership of indie brands if independence is a priority.

4. **Fragrance licenses:** Many luxury fashion houses (Gucci, Valentino, etc.) license fragrance production to conglomerates but retain brand ownership. Listed under the manufacturer (e.g., Gucci fragrance under Coty) for consumer clarity.

5. **Regional variations:** Some brands have different owners in different regions (e.g., Wella sold partial stake to KKR but Coty retains presence).
